## Why Visual Notes Work

Before diving into tips, let's understand why visual notes are so powerful:

- **65% of people are visual learners**
- **Visuals are processed 60,000x faster** than text
- **Information retention improves by 42%** with visual aids
- **Dual coding theory**: Combining words and images creates stronger memories

## 10 Tips for Better Visual Notes

### 1. Start with Structure

Before adding visuals, organize your content:

- Identify the main topic
- List 3-5 key points
- Group related information

**With NoteDraw**: Our AI Organizer does this automatically!

### 2. Use Visual Hierarchy

Not all information is equal. Show importance through:

- **Size**: Bigger = more important
- **Position**: Top/center for key points
- **Color**: Bold colors for emphasis

### 4. Create Visual Connections

Show relationships between ideas:

- **Arrows**: Direction or flow
- **Lines**: Connections
- **Containers**: Grouping related items
- **Proximity**: Closer = related

### 5. Limit Your Color Palette

Too many colors create chaos. Use:

- **2-3 main colors** for consistency
- **One accent color** for emphasis
- **Consistent meaning**: e.g., red = warning

### 6. Leave White Space

Don't overcrowd your notes:

- Breathing room improves readability
- Makes important points stand out
- Reduces cognitive load

### 7. Use Consistent Styles

Pick a visual style and stick with it:

- Hand-drawn for casual learning
- Clean lines for professional content
- Colorful for creative subjects

### 8. Include Examples

Abstract concepts become concrete with examples:

- Add mini-illustrations
- Include real-world applications
- Show before/after comparisons

### 9. Review and Revise

Visual notes improve with iteration:

- First pass: Capture key points
- Second pass: Add visuals
- Third pass: Refine and simplify

### 10. Practice Regularly

Like any skill, visual note-taking improves with practice:

- Start with simple topics
- Gradually increase complexity
- Experiment with different styles
